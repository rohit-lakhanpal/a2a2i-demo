# Southern Community Collective Inc.

## Capability Statement

---

### Who We Are

Southern Community Collective is a registered charity and volunteer-led community organisation based in Brighton, in Melbourne's Bayside area. Since 2011, we have delivered food access, social connection, and community wellbeing programs for local residents experiencing disadvantage.

We are incorporated under Consumer Affairs Victoria, registered with the ACNC, and governed by a volunteer Board of Directors with skills in finance, community services, legal, and organisational governance.

---

### What We Deliver

**Community Kitchen**
Weekly shared meals at Brighton Community Hall serving 60–80 community members per session. We use rescued food from OzHarvest and donated produce to prepare nutritious, culturally inclusive meals. The program is led by our Kitchen Coordinator (Maria Rossi) with support from 15 regular volunteers. This program has operated continuously since 2013.

**Pantry Share**
Fortnightly free food hamper service supporting approximately 120 households per cycle. Hampers include fresh fruit and vegetables, pantry staples, and culturally appropriate items. We partner with OzHarvest, Foodbank Victoria, and local supermarkets for food sourcing. Self-referral model — no means testing, no eligibility forms.

**Food Literacy Workshops**
Monthly cooking and nutrition workshops delivered in partnership with Bayside Community Health. Workshops focus on budget-friendly meals, reducing food waste, and healthy eating tailored for specific cohorts including older people and culturally diverse communities.

**Neighbour Connect**
Social connection program linking isolated residents with community activities, volunteer opportunities, and support services. Recognises that food insecurity is often connected to social isolation and broader disadvantage.

### Who We Serve

- Older residents on aged pensions and fixed incomes
- Single-parent families
- Renters experiencing housing stress in the Bayside area
- Socially isolated individuals, particularly older men and recently widowed people
- Culturally diverse community members including recent migrants
