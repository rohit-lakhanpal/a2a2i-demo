# Southern Community Collective Inc.

## Strategic Plan Summary — FY2024–2027

*Endorsed by the Board of Directors, September 2023*

---

### Our Vision

A connected Bayside community where no one goes hungry or faces hardship alone.

### Our Mission

Strengthening community wellbeing in Melbourne's Bayside region through practical support, connection, and food access programs.

---

### Strategic Context

Southern Community Collective has experienced steady growth in demand for our food programs since 2020. While the City of Bayside is generally an affluent area, we serve a growing number of residents facing quiet disadvantage — older people on aged pensions, single-parent households, renters experiencing housing stress, and socially isolated community members.

Our Community Kitchen and Pantry Share programs are well-established, trusted, and valued by participants. However, **rising operational costs are now the single greatest threat to our sustainability**. Our kitchen facility lease increased 18% in FY24, and retaining our Kitchen Coordinator — the backbone of our food programs — remains our most pressing funding challenge.

Without stable funding for these foundational costs, we face difficult decisions about reducing service frequency or losing experienced staff.

---

### Strategic Priorities

#### Priority 1: Sustain and Secure Core Food Programs *(Highest Priority)*

**Objective:** Ensure the long-term viability of Community Kitchen and Pantry Share by stabilising funding for core delivery costs.

**Key Actions:**
- **Secure grant funding to cover the Kitchen Coordinator salary ($68,000 p.a.) and Brighton Community Hall facility lease ($24,000 p.a.)** to relieve pressure on our general operating budget
- Maintain weekly Community Kitchen meals (60–80 participants per session) and fortnightly Pantry Share hampers (~120 households)
- Retain and strengthen existing partnerships with OzHarvest and St Andrew's Church for food rescue and venue access
- Explore multi-year funding arrangements to reduce annual grant dependency

> *This is our highest priority. These staff and facility costs are non-negotiable for continued service delivery. We are actively seeking grant programs that can underwrite these essential operational expenses.*

#### Priority 2: Expand Food Literacy and Wellbeing Programs

**Objective:** Grow our Food Literacy Workshops to reach more participants and integrate wellbeing supports.

**Key Actions:**
- Increase Food Literacy Workshops from monthly to fortnightly delivery
- Partner with Bayside Community Health to co-deliver nutrition and budget counselling sessions
- Develop targeted workshops for older residents and culturally diverse communities
- Pilot a "Cook & Connect" program linking food literacy with social isolation reduction

#### Priority 3: Strengthen Volunteer Networks and Community Partnerships

**Objective:** Build a larger, more diverse volunteer base and deepen partnerships across the Bayside area.

**Key Actions:**
- Recruit 20 additional active volunteers by FY2026
- Establish formal partnership agreements with 3 new community organisations
- Develop a volunteer recognition and retention program
- Continue partnership with Sandringham College for student volunteer placements

---

### Geographic Focus

Our programs are delivered primarily in **Brighton, Hampton, and Sandringham** within the **City of Bayside**. We also accept participants from neighbouring suburbs including Elsternwick, Bentleigh, and Cheltenham.

Our community need is localised. We focus on the pockets of disadvantage that exist within our otherwise affluent catchment — residents who are often invisible in broad demographic data but who face genuine food insecurity.

---

### Financial Sustainability

**Current Revenue Mix (FY24–25):**

| Source | Percentage |
|--------|-----------|
| Council grants and community grants | 42% |
| Donations and fundraising | 31% |
| Fee-for-service workshops | 15% |
| In-kind contributions | 12% |

**Key Financial Risk:** Our dependence on securing grants to cover core operational costs (staff wages, facility lease) means any gap in funding directly impacts service delivery. Each year, we must secure approximately $92,000 in grants just to maintain current operations — before any growth or new programs.

**Mitigation Strategy:** Diversify income through expanded workshop fees, corporate partnerships, and a planned end-of-year giving campaign. Seek multi-year grants where available. Invest in volunteer capacity to reduce paid labour dependency where appropriate.
