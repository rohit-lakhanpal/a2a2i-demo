# Southern Community Collective Inc.

## Annual Report Summary — FY2024–25

*For the year ended 30 June 2025*

---

### Chair's Message

This past year has reinforced both the importance and the fragility of community-led food programs. Demand for our Community Kitchen meals and Pantry Share hampers continued to rise — up 14% and 11% respectively — while our operating costs grew faster than our revenue. The Board and team have worked hard to maintain service quality in a challenging funding environment.

I want to acknowledge our incredible volunteers — over 45 people who gave more than 8,200 hours this year — and our dedicated staff team led by CEO Sarah Chen. Their commitment to our community is the foundation of everything we do.

**Margaret O'Brien**
*Chair, Board of Directors*

### Program Highlights

**Community Kitchen**
Our weekly Tuesday meals at Brighton Community Hall regularly served 60–80 people per session, up from 50–65 the previous year. We maintained our partnership with OzHarvest, receiving weekly food rescue collections, and expanded our culturally diverse menu options in response to participant feedback. Kitchen Coordinator Maria Rossi and her team of 15 regular volunteers continue to deliver exceptional quality and warmth.

**Pantry Share**
Our fortnightly hamper service supported approximately 120 households per cycle. We introduced culturally appropriate hamper options and improved our fresh produce sourcing through a new arrangement with a local greengrocer. Demand consistently exceeded supply — we maintained a waitlist of 15–25 households throughout the year.

**Food Literacy Workshops**
Monthly workshops delivered in partnership with Bayside Community Health attracted growing attendance. Highlights included a four-week "Cooking on a Budget" series for single parents and a pilot intergenerational cooking program with Sandringham College students and older community members.

**Neighbour Connect**
Our social connection program linked 65 isolated residents with community activities and volunteer opportunities. Participant satisfaction surveys reported 87% of participants felt "more connected to their community" after six months in the program.

### Key Challenges

1. **Rising operational costs** — Facility lease and staff costs increased significantly. Our Kitchen Coordinator role alone represents 18% of total expenditure, and the Brighton Community Hall lease jumped from $32,000 to $38,000. These core costs must be covered regardless of program activity levels.

2. **Demand outpacing capacity** — Pantry Share waitlists and Community Kitchen attendance growth signal unmet need. We are limited by both funding and volunteer capacity.

3. **Grant dependency for core costs** — We rely on grants to cover approximately 29% of our total revenue. Most grant programs fund project activities rather than ongoing operational costs, creating an annual challenge to cover staff wages and facility expenses.

---

### Looking Ahead — FY2025–26 Priorities

- Stabilise funding for core operations, particularly Kitchen Coordinator salary and facility lease
- Expand Food Literacy Workshops to fortnightly delivery
- Launch the "Cook & Connect" pilot combining food literacy with social connection
- Grow active volunteer base to 55+
- Explore corporate partnership opportunities for sustainable revenue
